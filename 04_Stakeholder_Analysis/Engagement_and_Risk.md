# Engagement and Risk Analysis

## Engagement Strategy

| STAKEHOLDER | STRATEGY | FREQUENCY | COMMUNICATION | OWNER |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Operations Management | Manage Closely | Weekly | Project status reports, KPI dashboards | Project Lead |
| Production Operators | Keep Satisfied / Consult | Daily | Shift huddles, visual boards, training | Production Supervisor |
| Maintenance Team | Manage Closely | Daily / Weekly | Planning meetings, root cause analysis sessions | Project Lead / Maintenance Mgr |
| Quality Control Team | Keep Informed / Consult | Weekly | Quality review meetings, defect logs | QC Lead |
| Supply Chain / Planning Team | Keep Informed | Bi-Weekly | Production schedule updates, inventory reports | Planning Manager |
| Distributors / Retailers | Monitor | Monthly | Customer satisfaction surveys, account management | Sales Team |

## Risk

| STAKEHOLDER | RISK TO PROJECT | RISK LEVEL | REASON | MITIGATION STRATEGY |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Production Operators | Resistance to new changeover procedures | High | Habitual work patterns; fear of increased workload | Involve them in designing the new procedures; provide clear training |
| Maintenance Team | Inability to fix underlying machine issues | Medium | Lack of parts or specialized knowledge | Ensure budget for necessary parts; schedule dedicated downtime for major fixes |
| Supply Chain / Planning Team | Scheduling too many short runs | High | Pressure from sales to meet all immediate demands | Establish a minimum run-size policy during the transition period |
| Operations Management | Pulling resources for other emergencies | Low | Shifting priorities | Clearly tie project KPIs to plant financial targets |
